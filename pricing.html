Pricing

Pricing is subject to current lumber prices

Wood availability can sometimes be circumvented through substitution but this comes at a higher cost (usually)
Interestingly the pressure treated stuff costs less right now than untreated wood.
Options ("Screws" or "Joinery") Denote options for joint style in the table base.
(Choosing the screws option means that the table is screwed together)

Recommended groupings:
  • Ultimate Table: Treated table (joinery), sanded table top
    • Longest lasting (possible 5+ years)
    • Minimal possible water damage during winter
    • Sturdy enough to use as a stage
    • smooth professional finish
    • can be stained for a luxury look or primed and painted to last even longer
  • Strong Base: Treated table (screws)
    • Quite strong
    • good looking almost stain like finish
    • should last multiple years
  • Cheap and easy: Light table, End shelf
    • Most inexpensive yet functional table
    • much more suseptable to water damage than the treated tables. priming can help mitigate this risk
